The Himalayas

468 peaks. A century of climbing. The highest place on Earth.

The Conquest

Starting in 1921, expeditions tried every face of the mountain. It took 32 years, dozens of lives, and routes from all sides before anyone reached the top.

The Turn

Exploration turned into commerce. By 2010, trekking agencies ran 98% of expeditions. More people summit each year. But the death toll stays flat.

The Cost

957 climbers never came back. Each dot is one death, placed at the altitude where it happened.

Where you die depends on why you're there. Hired staff fall in the icefall and lower camps. Clients collapse near the top.

On Everest, the divide is sharper. 2,400 meters between the typical hired staff death and the typical client death.

Hired staff death rate: 2.0%. Clients: 1.3%. Same mountain, different price.
